Anti-Scam Pokies Guide Australia

This page explains how to recognise suspicious pokies-related links, fake platform references, imitation websites, and misleading contact sources that may appear online.

If a page, message, or support contact does not look consistent with an official listing, do not rely on it without verification.

Common Warning Signs

Users should be cautious if they encounter any of the following:

  • Website links that do not match the official reference format
  • Contacts claiming to represent support without visible official confirmation
  • Messages pressuring immediate action or urgent payment steps
  • Poorly copied pages that look incomplete or inconsistent
  • Access points shared through unknown or unverified sources

How to Verify a Website

The safest approach is to compare the link against an official directory listing and confirm whether the company, support direction, and page structure match what is publicly presented.

If a reference appears unusual, users should stop and review the official support or safety pages before proceeding further.

What to Do if Something Looks Wrong

Do not continue with the page or contact source. Record the link, take a screenshot if needed, and report the matter through the recognised support contact for review.

Early reporting is always safer than waiting for a suspicious issue to become more serious.
